### Premise (draft)

A military veteran returns to her late aunt's rural Kentucky mountain property only to find it destroyed and besieged by a shifter pack claiming her land. Forced to rebuild alongside unlikely allies, she must untangle a rival pack's hidden agenda, confront the territorial Alpha Sheriff who wants her gone, and come to terms with a truth about herself she's only beginning to glimpse — one that could make her either their greatest threat or their last hope.

### Style notes (voice direction for AI)

Short-line, fragment-heavy prose. "Not X. Y." rhythm for emphasis. Military-precise observation. Restrained emotion — the protagonist doesn't narrate her feelings, she narrates what she notices. Third-person past tense is default (watch for drift into present in draft — Polish mode should catch). Physical presence conveyed through action and restraint, not adjective lists. Humor is dry, offhand, almost never signposted.

## Magic & World Rules

### Magic rules (7)

**1. Humans unaware**  
Paranormals live hidden. Exposure is a threat. Anyone who sees too much is a liability.  
*Cost:* n/a · *Limits:* n/a · *Known by:* all paranormals

**2. Magic costs the body**  
Every use of power exacts a physical toll — fatigue, injury, or worse. Limits are learned by pushing them.  
*Cost:* physical · *Limits:* individual · *Known by:* paranormals

**3. Shifter hierarchy: alpha / beta / omega**  
Traditional pack structure. Rank carries obedience weight — an alpha's command is instinctively felt, not just heard. *(Narrative hook: ch. 1 established Zoe's resistance to Wyatt's* "Step outside" *command — she either has her own authority or is not bound by shifter instinct.)*  
*Known by:* shifters, most paranormals aware of shifter culture

**4. Mating bonds exist — refusal is clean**  
True-mate bonds form between shifters. They can be refused with no physical or emotional cost to either party. *(Craft implication: tension is "will they choose each other," not "will refusal destroy them.")*  
*Known by:* shifters

**5. Shifters are born, not made**  
No turning via bite. Shifter nature is inherited through lineage. *(Critical implication: Zoe is not a shifter, regardless of what Wyatt smells on her. Her nature is something else entirely.)*  
*Known by:* shifters

**6. Classic shifter weaknesses**  
Silver wounds and poisons shifters. Wolfsbane is toxic. Moon phases affect the shift — likely compulsory transformation or heightened aggression at full moon.  
*Known by:* shifters, hunters, most paranormals

**7. Species coexist territorially**  
Paranormal species operate on guard with each other. Territory matters. Tension is the baseline state; peace is earned through treaty, proximity, or blood.  
*Known by:* all paranormals

## Appendix A — Zoe's hidden-nature constraints

Established behaviors (from ch. 1), stored as `character.behaviors_established`. Her species can be deferred; any species she chooses must satisfy all three:

1. Commands fire with intent when emotionally triggered
2. Scent reads as "summer fire" / unrecognized by shifters
3. Heals others unconsciously (at minimum when powerful emotion is involved)

**Species candidates that fit the constraints:**

- **Phoenix-blooded** — fire affinity, regenerative/healing, ancient rare lineage
- **Fire elemental / salamander-descended** — direct fire command, warmth-associated scent
- **Djinn-descended** — fire origin, granted-wish / intent-driven magic
- **Sun-blessed / solar-touched** — radiant healing, fire through emotion
- **Fae offshoot (summer court)** — emotional magic, seasonal scent, ancient bloodline
- **Celestial / divine-descended** — rare, unknown to most species, healing gift

No decision needed yet. The mystery deepens as ch. 2–6 add behaviors; the reveal lands whenever the black moment or grand gesture calls for it.
